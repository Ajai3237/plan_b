Lead Training Service

Lead Hazard Awareness Training

Course Type: In person

Course Length: ½ Day

Why Do I Need This Training?

Lead is a highly toxic material that can cause a multitude of severe health effects including neurological disorders, gastrointestinal effects, anemia, and kidney disease. Workers in construction and industrial settings are often exposed to lead occupationally due to a lack of understanding of what activities may lead to lead exposure and poor worksite hygiene practices. Being able to identify products that may contain lead and understand the work practices that may exposure workers to lead, is essential for worker protection.


When Do I Require This Training?

The Occupational Health & Safety Act requires employers to make workers aware of the hazards in their workplace and take every reasonable precaution in the circumstances for the protection of the worker.


If you are an employer or a worker at a workplace where lead containing materials are present, produced, processed, used, handled, or stored this course will educate you on how to identify potential lead containing materials, as well as how to navigate any applicable legislation and guidelines to deal with the classification of lead operations.


Who Should Attend?
  • Any workers, including construction tradespeople, who work in environments where they may encounter lead containing products in their work.
  • Supervisors of workers who may encounter lead containing products in their work.
  • Individuals who contract out work on lead containing materials.
  • Health and Safety Professionals and Joint Health and Safety Committee members looking to expand their knowledge.

Please Note: the scope of this course does not include the requirements for performing Class 1, 2 or 3 lead operations. If you are conducting work activities that may expose workers to lead (e.g. surface coating removal, welding/burning, demolition / construction / restoration work), please refer to our “Lead Hazard Awareness and Class 1 Operations Worker Training” for more detailed training for Class 1 Operations workers. If you are unsure which training is appropriate for your employees, please contact us for guidance.


What Will Be Covered?

This course will include training focused on:


  • Lead properties, types and applications (historical and current).
  • Health effects of lead exposure.
  • Lead Legislation (Ontario Regulations 490/09 & 213/91, MLITSD Lead on Construction Projects Guideline, Environmental Abatement Council of Canada (EACC) Lead Guideline for Construction, Renovation, Maintenance or Repair).
  • Classifying Lead Operations (Class 1, 2 and 3).

The course will also include completion of program worksheets and practical exercises around interpretation of the legislation and identification of lead containing products.


What’s Included?

Each course participant will receive:


  • Participant guide including course slides with areas for note taking.
  • Program worksheets to complete in class.

Will I Receive a Certificate?

At the end of the training, a performance evaluation (multiple choice test) will be administered to determine the comprehension and information retention level of the participant. Participants will also be asked to complete program worksheets during the course to assist in evaluating content retention. There is no pass/fail criteria assigned to this program. As such, all participants will be issued a certificate/wallet card acknowledging completion provided by Norpro H&S.
